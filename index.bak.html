A Peek Behind the Curtain

The Chomsky Insight

In 1965, Noam Chomsky revolutionized linguistics with a discovery: all human languages—despite wildly different vocabularies and surface rules—share identical deep structure. A universal grammar hardwired into human cognition.

COMPASS™ applies this principle to business operations.

Universal Process Grammar

Every organization believes its processes are unique. Healthcare thinks it's different from finance. Government thinks it's different from manufacturing. They're wrong.

Beneath the surface terminology, all processes share the same deep structure. We call this Universal Process Grammar (UPG)—the discovery that every business process, regardless of industry, conforms to six universal principles:

Principle What It Means
Completeness Every process has a defined beginning, middle, and end
Hierarchy Work organizes into nested levels (Pathway → Milestone → Job → Task → Step)
Conditionality Variations branch based on specific variables
Exception Handling Every process accounts for when things go wrong
Actor Assignment Every action has a designated performer
Nomenclature Consistent naming conventions across all process elements

This isn't theory. It's 25 years of proven methodology across banking, healthcare, and federal government—now embedded in AI.

Why This Matters

Surface structure varies by domain: Healthcare says "Verify Insurance Eligibility." Finance says "Validate Account Status." Government says "Confirm Benefit Eligibility."

Deep structure is identical: Access system → Query with identifier → Validate response → Record result → Handle exceptions.

COMPASS™ captures the deep structure. When your SME describes "how we verify insurance," COMPASS™ recognizes the universal pattern beneath the domain-specific language.
